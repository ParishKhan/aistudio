OpenAI moved the GPT-5.6 family to general availability on July 9, introducing
durable capability tiers: **Sol** (flagship), **Terra** (balanced), and
**Luna** (cost-efficient). All three share a 1,050,000-token context window,
128K max output, and a February 16, 2026 knowledge cutoff.

The headline numbers are efficiency claims. On the Artificial Analysis Coding
Agent Index, [GPT-5.6 Sol](/gpt-5-6-sol) posts a state-of-the-art 80 — 2.8
points above Claude Fable 5 — while using less than half the output tokens.
Terra beats the previous flagship GPT-5.5 on most coding evals at half the
price, and Luna nearly matches GPT-5.5's knowledge-work peak at one-fifth the
cost.

Also new: Programmatic Tool Calling in the Responses API (the model writes
in-memory programs that coordinate tools), a multi-agent beta, an "ultra"
setting that runs four parallel agents, and explicit prompt-cache breakpoints
with a 30-minute minimum cache life.

Pricing is unchanged at the top: Sol matches GPT-5.5's $5/$30 per 1M tokens.
See the [full comparison against Claude Opus 4.8](/gpt-5-6-sol-vs-claude-opus-4-8)
and [against Claude Fable 5](/gpt-5-6-sol-vs-claude-fable-5).
